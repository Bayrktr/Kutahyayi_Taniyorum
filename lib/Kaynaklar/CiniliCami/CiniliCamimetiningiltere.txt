It is in the city center in Maltepe neighborhood. It was built in 1973 by the famous artist and ney player Ahmet Yakupoğlu of Kütahya. With its building style and ornamentation style, it carries inspirations from Central Asian Turkish architecture. 
The interior of the octagonal two-storey mosque with a single dome is decorated with hand-drawn ornaments, and the outside is decorated with specially produced blue Kütahya tiles. It is one of the must-see symbol works of Kütahya with its decorations and architecture using Turkish motifs.
